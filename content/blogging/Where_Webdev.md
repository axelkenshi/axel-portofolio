## The turning point

For months, I struggled to really grasp things as I bounced between different courses—ranging from long-winded, free local bootcamps to local YouTube tutorials that I felt lacked depth in their explanations.

I eventually reached a point of frustration regarding what I was doing.

Honestly, I felt like I was just spinning my wheels in a cycle of tutorial hells, trapped at a beginner level where I needed to be spoon-fed information.

Then, at one point—while bored and browsing through thousands of YouTube videos—a playlist on full-stack JavaScript web development by someone named Huxn popped up; I’m glad it did after watch.

I watched the first series on web development, and it was incredibly easy to follow; even though I’m not fluent in English, the explanations were clear and easy to understand for a non-native speaker.

## Ah, I've found some hidden gem

Over time, however, I felt that relying solely on video courses—which provided information but didn't always lead to true understanding—left me lacking in terms of hands-on, "do-it-yourself" (DIY) challenges.

I needed something to fill those knowledge gaps. I came across a recommendation on a forum for a 100% free, curated, text-based self-study course called "The Odin Project."

The material there was similar to what I had found on YouTube, but it also presented challenges and content that forced me to figure things out for myself.

This was crucial for developing problem-solving soft skills and getting accustomed to reading technical documentation—the kind written with the assumption that the reader is a fellow programmer.

I took pride in completing both approaches; for me, this became the perfect recipe for learning web development—combining human-friendly explanations with technical ones.

My advice so far is to learn in the way that suits you best: don't get stuck on a single source, and explore other resources that you find valuable.

After all, the deeper your mastery becomes, the fewer resources there are available to learn from.
